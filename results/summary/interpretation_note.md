## Scientific Interpretation Note

The phi-lattice signals detected by LatticeFit across domains do NOT
imply a common underlying mechanism. The correct interpretation by domain:

PHYSICS (SM fermion masses):
  - Derived from arithmetic geometry of Q(sqrt(5)) unit lattice
  - Spacing log(phi) = regulator of Q(sqrt(5)), not a free parameter
  - Independent predictions: mixing matrices, CP phase, covering tower
  - This is a theoretical framework, not post-hoc fitting

BIOLOGY (rice MAF, cetacean masses):
  - Empirical clustering near phi-lattice
  - Mechanism unknown - could reflect evolutionary optimization,
    demographic constraints, or allometric scaling laws
  - Requires independent mechanistic explanation
  - Statistical signal is real but interpretation is open

PHARMACOLOGY (EGFR SAR):
  - Marginal signal (z~+2, n~32), borderline significant
  - Could reflect compound series design (3-10x potency spacing)
  - Requires replication on larger series before interpretation

NULL RESULTS (finance, earthquakes, ionization energies):
  - These datasets are governed by known non-phi laws
  - GR law, random walk, quantum shell structure
  - LatticeFit correctly returns null - validates the method

ARTIFACTS (binned data, narrow range):
  - These are methodological failures, not phi-structure
  - Screened by validity criteria (>3 orders, non-binned)

The SM mass result motivates the mathematical framework.
The biological results motivate further investigation.
They should not be presented as manifestations of the same phenomenon.
